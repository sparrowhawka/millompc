Service Questions

These common questions directly relate to the services we offer. If anything's not listed, feel free to contact us.

Can you take away my computer for repairs (and return after repairs/upgrades)?

Yes - we operate a collect and return service if requested.

What does "Beyond Economic Repair" mean?

This is where the cost of fixing a device is more than the value of that device. For example if a laptop has extensive water damage there may be so little working it is, in our opinion irrepairable. We can still proceed with repairs if you want, but these are likely to be expensive and we cannot guarantee success.
If the device is beyond repair, we may still be able to recover data for you, though again this is not guaranteed.

Can you fix Apple computers?

Yes - most problems.
However, some newer models are harder to fix, because some Apple computers have parts that are normally removable fixed in place, or do not have access to them. We would advise if this is the case.
If your Apple device has a fault and is covered by Warranty or AppleCare, we strongly advise you to first seek advise directly from Apple, of from an Apple Authorised Service Provider.

Can you upgrade Apple computers?

Yes - many apple computers we can upgrade common components such as RAM and HDD to a SSD. However, some Apple computers are manufactured in such a way that the components are fixed in place and cannot be changed or upgraded. Please get in touch for advise on upgrades, and we will find out what's possible for you.

Pricing and Deposits

We're committed to keeping our prices as low as possible, and we never charge markup on spare parts, or invent additional 'surprise' fees.

Because every computer job is often unique we don't provide prices online, so please get in touch for a quote.

Why do spare parts require a deposit?

Because parts can be unique to your computer we cannot stock all the parts required to fix all computer problems, and may need to order them in. Expensive parts require a deposit because we may not have a use for them other than on your device.

We usually don't charge deposit on parts for the collect and return service.

Trade-in and Recycling

What can you accept for free, for recycling or spares?

We will accept:
Working or Faulty:
  • Desktop Computers (Towers)
  • Laptops
  • Components and Parts
We will sometimes be able to collect these items outside of Millom, please get in touch to confirm.

We will also accept the following, providing they are in working order:
  • Computer Mice and Keyboards
  • Computer Speaker Systems
  • Flat-screen Screens and Monitors
  • Old phones and tablets
  • Cables and Chargers
Currently, we are able to only offer collection of these items in Millom.

We cannot accept the following:
  • CRT 'Bulky'/'Old-style' Computer Monitors

What happens to items donated to recycling?

We believe in re-use wherever possible. Working or repairable computers may be refurbished for re-sale, and broken computers and laptops will be stripped of all useful parts which will be retained. Items which are of no use to us, we will advertise on the Freecycle network and similar websites. Anything else will be recycled responsibly.

Can I sell you my old computer?

We will sometimes be able to buy an old computer off you either for rebfurbishment for eventual re-sale, or to strip down for parts. If we are interested, we will make an offer for your machine, which is subject to inspection and verification - e.g. it is the specification you advise.
The offer we are able to make represents the value of the device to us (and of course - you are not obliged to accept it).

Can I trade in my old computer against the cost of services?

Sometimes you can trade in your old computer against the cost of services. However, this is subject to prior arrangement and inspection of the trade-in items.
Please be aware that we are not obliged to accept a trade-in instead of payment.
Trade-in and payment for services are viewed as 2 separate transactions even if of equal value.
