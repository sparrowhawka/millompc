General Questions

These are all common questions relating to our services!

Where do your call outs cover?

Our call out service primarily covers Millom and the immediate surroundings (including Haverigg).
We will come further out (e.g. to rural locations), but these call-outs may be subject to a small additional charge to cover petrol costs. We will of course advise you of this beforehand if it applies.

Do you have a physical shop?

No, sorry.
We operate on an on-line and call out based service at present.

What are your operating hours?

Call outs currently take place between 11:00am and 8:30pm, weekdays.
We have taliored our working hours so that we can come to you outside standard working hours (avoiding the need for you to take the day of work for computer repairs!). 'After hours' appointments may be subject to high demand.
We may occasionally be able to arrange weekend repairs, though you will have to contact us directly about this (subject to availability).

How can we be contacted, and what is the fastest method to get in touch?

We can be contacted through our Facebook page, by email (info@millomcomputers.co.uk), and also by text or phone. We aim to respond within one working day, though please be aware that mobile reception can often be poor.
The fastest way to get in touch is generally through the facebook page or email, as these are most closely monitored.

Do you fix TV's and other equipment?

No. We are only able to diagnose and fix computers at present. This includes Microsoft, as well as some Apple computers (For more information on Apple devices see repair FAQ section).

Do you fix phones and tablets?

Normally no, though this actually depends on the problem. We can fix some problems with phones and tablets, but we do not undertake hardware repairs such as water damage or cracked screens. If in any doubt, please get in touch if you aren't sure and we will advise.

Service Questions

These common questions directly relate to the services we offer. If anything's not listed, feel free to contact us.

Can you take away my computer for repairs (and return after repairs/upgrades)?

Yes - if you prefer we can often collect devices for repair and bring them back once we have completed the repair or upgrade. Unfortunately we don't have a shop premesis though.

What is 'BER' - Beyond Economic Repair?

This is where the cost of fixing a device is more than the value of that device. For example if a laptop has extensive water damage there may be so little working it is, in our opinion irrepairable. We can still proceed with repairs if you want, but these are likely to be expensive and we cannot gaurentee sucess.
If the device is beyond repair, we may still be able to recover data for you, though again this is not gurenteed.

Can you fix Apple computers?

Yes - most problems.
However, some newer models are harder to fix hardware problems, because some Apple computers have parts that are normally removeable fixed in place, or do not have access to them. We would advise if this is the case.
If your Apple device has a fault and is covered by Warranty or AppleCare, we strongly advise you to first seek advise directly from Apple, of from an Apple Authorised Service Provider.

Can you upgrade Apple computers?

Yes - many apple computers we can upgrade common components such as RAM and HDD to a SSD. However, some Apple computers are manufactured in such a way that the components are fixed in place and cannot be changed or upgraded. Please get in touch for advise on upgrades, and we will find out what's possible for you.

Deposits

Parts

If we have to order in spare parts, you may have to pay a parts deposit. This is typically 40% of the cost of the parts required. Particularly rare or expensive parts may be subject to a higher deposit.

If you prefer, you can order the parts yourself. If you do, we only charge the diagnostic and fitting fees.

Mileage / Travel Charge

We don't specify a mileage rate on our invoices, but call outs outside of Millom and Haverigg may cost slightly more due to the inccured travel costs.

Why can't exact prices for diagnostics and repair be quoted on the website?

Many computer faults (and their fixes) are unique - and therefore take different amounts of time and resoruces to sort out. We give an idea of price 'average' price for these repairs generally, so people know what to expect - we find it's more helpful than no idication of price at all, and it will apply to the majority of fixes.

You have determined my computer needs replacement parts which requires a second visit. Will I be charged for the cost of two visits?

No.
You will be only charged one overall service charge plus the cost of parts. The service charge would generally not be double the price of a single visit.

Parts may require a deposit, or alternatively we can advise you what to order yourself and only charge the service fee.

If you do order your own parts, you will be charged the fee for diagnostics at the time of the first visit, and the remainder 'fitting fee', on the return visit. We do this to prevent people utilising our diagnostic service free of charge and then getting the repair completed elsewhere.

Why do spare parts require a deposit?

Because parts can be unique to your computer we cannot stock all the parts required to fix all computer problems. Similarly, we often cannot use parts for specific computers on others, so we require a deposit to ensure we don't make a loss ordering parts which are then not used.
The alternative is, you can order the parts yourself and let us know when they have arrived, and we will fit them.

Do you charge markup on parts?

Definately not! You pay the same as we do for parts. We can also tell you which parts you need so you can order them yourself if you prefer.
Ordering your own parts is often helpful because it means that you have the warranty from the supplier in your name. We can explain this more if you want us to!

Trade-in and Recycling

What can you accept for free, for recycling or spares?

We will accept:
Working or Faulty:
  • Desktop Computers (Towers)
  • Laptops
  • Components and Parts
We will sometimes be able to collect these items outside of Millom, please get in touch to confirm.

We will also accept the following, providing they are in working order:
  • Computer Mice and Keyboards
  • Computer Speaker Systems
  • Flat-screen Screens and Monitors
  • Old phones and tablets
  • Cables and Chargers
Currently, we are able to only offer collection of these items in Millom.

We cannot accept the following:
  • CRT 'Bulky'/'Old-style' Computer Monitors

What happens to items donated to recycling?

We believe in re-use whereever possible. Working or repairable computers may be refurbished for re-sale, and broken computers and laptops will be stripped of all useful parts which will be retained. Items which are of no use to us, we will advertise on the Freecycle network and similar websites. Anything else will be recycled responsibly.

Can I sell you my old computer?

We will sometimes be able to buy an old computer off you either for rebfurbishement for eventaul re-sale, or to strip down for parts. If we are interested, we will make an offer for your machine, which is subject to inspection and verficiation - e.g. it is the specification you advise.
The offer we are able to make represents the value of the device to us (and of course - you are not obliged to accept it).

Can I trade in my old computer against the cost of services?

Sometimes you can trade in your old computer against the cost of services. However, this is subject to prior arrangement and inspection of the trade-in items.
Please be aware that we are not obliged to accept a trade-in instead of payment.
Trade-in and payment for services are viewed as 2 seperate transactions even if of equal value.
